Aviation technician

An aviation technician, also known as an aircraft mechanic, is a skilled tradesperson who maintains and repairs aircraft. To become one, you typically need a high school diploma, mechanical aptitude, and must attend an FAA-approved aviation maintenance technician school or complete an apprenticeship to earn an Airframe and Powerplant (A&P) license. Their duties include inspecting aircraft systems, troubleshooting issues, performing repairs, and ensuring airworthiness for safety.

Responsibilities and Duties

  • Inspection and Maintenance: Conduct routine inspections to check for any issues, wear, or damage on aircraft, including engines, airframe, hydraulics, and avionics.
  • Troubleshooting: Diagnose and identify the root cause of problems with aircraft systems.
  • Repairs and Alterations: Perform maintenance, repairs, and alterations to aircraft components and systems
  • Documentation: Keep meticulous records of all maintenance performed and ensure compliance with regulations.
